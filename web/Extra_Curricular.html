EXTRA-CURRICULAR





Discover Your Passions in Ten Unique Avenues




Every Club & Society at Galahitiyawa central College has something unique to offer to its member base, whether it be kindling their passion towards specific interests, furthering their ability to work in unity or even being a role model to peers. There are over 45 active student organizations at the College, all of which promise different invaluable lessons and experiences that have produced countless all-round characters.
The school takes great pride in the service provided to every section of society by the Clubs & Societies it offers. With 10 avenues to accommodate the wide variety of endeavours undertaken by the organizations, there is a Club or Society to drive forth the advancement of every aspect of the College and the nation.

Photography society

Founded in 1934 as the Photographic Art Society of Galahitiyawa Central College, it later changed the name to Royal College Photographic Society. However, for a decade since 1954 the society was temporarily inactive. After beginning anew in the year 1964 The Photographic Society was able to organize the first Inter-school Photographic Exhibition in Sri Lanka.

Prefect Society

Prefects should act as the role model for all the students in the School. Prefects must adhere to School Rules and Regulations at all times. The main duty of prefects is to maintain an atmosphere of friendly cooperation, peace, discipline and unity in the School. Prefects should serve as counselors to junior students.

Media Society

Providing media coverage for all the activities in the school. Training students for the announcing activities and directing students to participate in the seminars hold by the media.At the situation we have taken steps to provide information of the special events to the media.

AESTHETIC SOCIETIES




The Art Circle of Galahitiyawa College has been one of the most active societies of Royal College since 1995. It is also one of the largest clubs in the school, with a member base of over 1000 students. This society has always played a main role in polishing the aesthetic talents of not only Centralianz but also of Young students from all over the island. The main duty of those at the Art Circle is to develop the student’s talents to an extent of being recognized by other people in the society. Also, the Art Circle works to pave a path for those talented students who wish to carry out their skills in the future.

Music

The Galahitiyawa central College Music Society was first formed in November 1919 by acting principal Lewis Walker, Esq. as the College Music Society making it one of the first music-based organizations in the country. The Society’s main annual finance project and public event Festival of Choirs made its debut in the year 1993. It features the best of Sri Lankan School Choirs and has grown in reputation as an event eagerly awaited each year by Sri Lanka’s youth choral music sphere.

Dancing

The Dancing Society of Galahitiyawa Central College was formed in the year 2000. With the pioneering work of Mrs. Trishila Dissanayaka, the Dancing Society was able to start off as a student organization. For the first time in the College’s history a ‘Wes Mangalya’ was done in the year 2005 under the guidance of Mr. Sanath Abeykoon. ‘Thathjith Soul Expressions’ is the main project organized by the Dancing Society of Galahitiyawa Central College. The goal of the society is to experiment within the world of dance to find new methods and improve.

Drama

Founded in 2014 the Sinhala Drama Society aims to produce a playwright with a robust set of skills ranging from scriptwriting, directing, lighting, theatre management and decorating. Throughout the year the society focuses on enhancing the artistic talents of students within and outside school by conducting competitions, providing workshops and carrying out weekly development programs for budding actors.
